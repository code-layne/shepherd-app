lesson target

a.ei.1 the student will represent, solve, and interpret the solution to absolute value equations and inequalities in one variable.

  • b - solve an absolute value equation in one variable algebraically and verify the solution graphically
  • d - Solve an absolute value inequality in one variable and represent the solution set using set notation, interval notation, and using a number line

essential question

what are the similarities and differences in the procedures for solving and expressing the solutions of equations and inequalities?

success criteria

  • i can apply the properties of real numbers and properties of equality/inequality to simplify expressions and solve multistep linear equations and inequalities
  • i can solve multistep linear equations and inequalities in one variable algebraically involving:
    • \(\rightarrow\) rational numbers/expressions
    • \(\rightarrow\) distributing
    • \(\rightarrow\) combining like terms
    • \(\rightarrow\) variables on both sides
    • \(\rightarrow\) negative coefficients

warmup: solving equations and inequalities

solve the following equation/inequality and specify the solution or solution set

$$ \begin{aligned} 3x + 5 &= 17 \\ \\ 4x - 7 &\lt 9 \\ \\ \end{aligned} $$

hook: mystery number

i'm thinking of a number. if i triple it and add 5, i get 17. what's the number?

properties of equality/inequality
addition/subtraction property of equality/inequality $$ 3x^2 + 9x $$ $$ = 3x(x+3) $$
multiplication/division property of equality/inequality $$ x^2 +7x+12 $$ $$ = (x+4)(x+3) $$
other operations $$ 6x^2+11x-10 $$ $$ = (2x+5)(3x-2) $$
properties of real numbers
commutative property (addition/multiplication) $$ 3x^2 + 9x $$ $$ = 3x(x+3) $$
associative property (addition/multiplication) $$ x^2 +7x+12 $$ $$ = (x+4)(x+3) $$
distributive property $$ 6x^2+11x-10 $$ $$ = (2x+5)(3x-2) $$
identity property (addition/multiplication) $$ 6x^2+11x-10 $$ $$ = (2x+5)(3x-2) $$
inverse property (addition/multiplication) $$ 6x^2+11x-10 $$ $$ = (2x+5)(3x-2) $$
