lesson target

a.eo.2 the student will perform operations on and factor polynomial expressions in one variable.

  • c - factor completely first- and second-degree polynomials in one variable with integral coefficients. after factoring out the greatest common factor (gcf), leading coefficients should have no more than four factors.

essential question

how do polynomials relate to other algebraic concepts, such as factoring and quadratic equations?

success criteria

  • i can factor out the greatest common factor(gcf)
  • i can factor completely first and second degree polynomials in one variable with integral coefficients.

factoring
gcf $$ 3x^2 + 9x $$ $$ = 3x(x+3) $$
trinomial \(a=1\) $$ x^2 +7x+12 $$ $$ = (x+4)(x+3) $$
trinomial \(a\neq1\) $$ 6x^2+11x-10 $$ $$ = (2x+5)(3x-2) $$
difference of squares $$ x^2-36 $$ $$ = (x+6)(x-6)$$
square of a sum $$ (x+3)^2 $$ $$ = x^2+6x+9 $$
square of a difference $$ (x-3)^2 $$ $$ = x^2-6x+9 $$
factoring - step by step
  1. factor out gcf $$ 6x^{3}-12x-14x^{2} = 2x(3x^2-6-7x)$$
  2. put in standard form \(\rightarrow ax^2+bx+c\) $$ = 2x(3x^2-7x-6)$$
  3. find your two numbers \(x+y=b\) and \(xy=ab\) $$ x+y=-7 \quad xy=(3)(-6)=-18 \quad \rightarrow {-9,2} $$
  4. use your two numbers as coefficients of your middle terms $$ =2x(3x^2-9x+2x-6) $$
  5. factor by grouping - factor out the gcf of each pair of terms $$ =2x(3x(x-3)+2(x-3)) $$
  6. factor out the common binomial $$ =2x(3x+2)(x-3) $$
